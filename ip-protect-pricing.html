Why Blockchain Registration?

A smarter way to protect your IP

Traditional IP registries were designed for a paper world. Blockchain was built for the digital age — borderless, tamper-proof, and permanent.

🌍

No Territorial Limits

A blockchain record is visible and valid worldwide by default. Traditional registrations (INPI, DPMA, UKIPO…) only cover the country or region where you filed — you'd need separate applications to get global coverage.

♾️

Lifetime Validity — No Renewal

Once written on the blockchain, your registration exists permanently. Traditional registrations typically expire after 10 years and require renewal fees every decade to remain active.

⚡

Instant & Affordable

Your registration is confirmed in minutes, for a fraction of the cost. Filing with national offices involves application fees, examination periods that can take months or years, and often the cost of a legal representative.

🔒

Tamper-Proof by Design

Blockchain records cannot be altered, deleted, or forged after creation. Your timestamp is immutable proof of when you owned your work — something no paper certificate can guarantee at the same level.

⚖️

Legally Recognised Timestamp

Governments and courts in the EU and beyond increasingly recognise blockchain-timestamped evidence. The EU eIDAS Regulation (2014/910/EU) acknowledges the legal effect of electronic timestamps, and many jurisdictions accept blockchain proof as a date of creation.

📁

Register One or Many Works

You are not limited to a single item. Within your storage quota, you can register an entire portfolio — photos, music, writing, code, designs — all under one transaction.

🏛️ A note on legal recognition Multiple states and international courts accept blockchain records as probative evidence of prior creation and ownership. The EU's eIDAS framework, along with legislation in Switzerland, France, Malta, Liechtenstein, and others, provides a legal basis for blockchain timestamps in intellectual property disputes. While blockchain registration complements — rather than replaces — traditional copyright (which arises automatically on creation in most countries), it gives you the strongest possible timestamped proof the moment you publish or register your work.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before registering.

Is blockchain registration legally valid?

Yes. In the European Union, the eIDAS Regulation (2014/910/EU) recognises the legal effect of electronic timestamps. Several member states (France, Switzerland, Malta, Liechtenstein) and courts worldwide have accepted blockchain records as admissible evidence of prior creation and ownership. IP Protect gives you a court-ready proof of your work's existence at a precise moment in time.

Does blockchain registration replace a traditional patent or trademark?

No — and we don't claim it does. Blockchain registration is a powerful complement to traditional IP rights. It establishes proof of when you created your work, which is critical in copyright disputes. Patents and trademarks are separate legal instruments that confer specific exclusive rights; those still require official registration with the relevant authority.

Can I register multiple works in one plan?

Yes. Within your storage allowance (100 MB, 500 MB, or 1 GB), you can register as many individual works as you like. The combined file size of all your works must simply stay within your plan's limit.

What types of files can I register?

Any digital file — photographs, illustrations, music, audio recordings, videos, manuscripts, source code, architectural drawings, 3D models, PDFs, and more. If it is a digital file, IP Protect can register it.

Is my file stored permanently on the blockchain?

The blockchain stores a cryptographic hash (fingerprint) of your file — not the file itself. This fingerprint is a unique mathematical representation that proves the file existed at that exact moment. Your original file is securely stored on Divenci's servers for the lifetime of your registration.

What if I need more storage after purchasing a plan?

You can upgrade to a higher plan or purchase an additional registration at any time. Each registration has its own blockchain transaction and storage allowance.
